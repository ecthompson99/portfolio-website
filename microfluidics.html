Free Flow Electrophoresis

Continuous Protein Separation

Free-flow electrophoresis is a technique used to separate analytes such as DNA and proteins based on their electrophoretic mobilities; namely, their size and charge. Separation is performed to characterize the analytes' function, structure, and interactions. The exact details of the separation mechanism will remain confidential until our findings are published in early 2020. There are several advantages that are introduced when working at the micro scale. The process is less resource-intensive, and certain physical phenomena, like joule heating, are less prominent.

My role as a coop student, was to prove that our method was feasible. There were prototypes built before me, but none had shown separation. My goal was to build a device that could sufficiently fractionate food colouring dye.

The first few iterations were "learning opportunities", to say the least. I quickly learned how volatile microfluidic systems are; a single bubble in your microchannel can ruin your entire day. Fortunately, I was quick to put out those fires and eventually I implemented the electric field-generating electrodes. The separation was there, but it was messy. This is where I began using parametric studies to study the effect of the different parameters.
